Lonza floods – Step 4: combining rainfall and flow perspectives

Project hub: Estimating extreme floods in an alpine catchment

Two viewpoints on the same hazard

By this stage, we have:

  • A flow‑based estimate of the 100‑year peak discharge \(Q_{100}\) from extreme value analysis of the gauge record, and
  • A rainfall‑based estimate obtained by running the calibrated rainfall–runoff model with a 100‑year design storm.

Combining these viewpoints helps us check consistency and understand the sensitivity of our design values to modelling assumptions.

Comparing peaks and volumes

I compare:

  • The peak flows from both approaches,
  • The associated flood volumes (area under each synthetic hydrograph), and
  • The timing and shape of the hydrographs for different storm durations.

In my workflow, discrepancies are treated as information. If the rainfall‑based estimate is systematically higher, it may indicate that the flow record does not yet contain a “true” 100‑year event, or that the rainfall representation is conservative. If it is lower, the catchment might be able to concentrate runoff more efficiently than the simple lumped model suggests.
